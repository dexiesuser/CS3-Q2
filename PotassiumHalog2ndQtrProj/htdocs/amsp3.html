AM's Beneath the Boardwalk to The Car: A Deep Dive

Time Machine: 2007 vs 2013 AM, Two Different Eras

KNEE SOCKS. Just before the 'AM' album concludes, there's a song that might be its best. We're bouncing as the memories in Alex's head come together in a final crescendo, which begins with a bass drum and basic guitar riff. Lyrics like "When you strolled around your house wearing my sky blue Lacoste... And your knee socks" tempt listeners to melt into the offered emotions. This performance, despite it not being in a headliner rock arena, is one of my favorites. I appreciate the comfort of small concert gatherings, and the crowd's cheer is not too overwhelming. As for the delivery of the song, what more can I say? Arctic Monkeys will always deliver, especially on this track. The suave and lulling performance of the band always sweeps me off my feet.

FLUORESCENT ADOLESCENT. The ballad on their second album 'Favourite Worst Nightmare' is about growing older and realizing that life isn't as exciting as it once was. With an emphasis on how "rowdy" she was as a teenager, it tells the story of a lady who longs for her younger years because her monotonous and unfulfilling life today. Comparing this old recording to the previous 2013 recording I showed you before, you can see the change in the once pure-garage-rock-and-teenage-spirit band into the legends they are today. This performance, or rather the song as a whole, encapsulates teenage spirit the best for me. Not too rowdy, not too laidback. Also, this 'The Letterman Show' appearance will forever be iconic in my eyes.
